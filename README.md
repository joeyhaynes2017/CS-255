# DriverPass Project Summary
The DriverPass project focused on designing a system for a company called DriverPass. The client wanted an online system that would help students better prepare for their driver’s license exam and schedule behind-the-wheel training. The system would allow customers to create accounts, purchase training packages, complete online practice tests, schedule driving lessons, make payments, and track their progress. Employees also needed features to manage appointments, instructors, vehicles, customer information, and reports. Throughout the project, I analyzed the client’s requirements and created several UML diagrams and system design documents to demonstrate how the system would function.

# What I Did Well
One of the strongest parts of my work was identifying the system requirements and translating them into UML diagrams. I believe my use case, activity, and object diagrams accurately represented how different users interact with the system. I also improved my ability to think like a systems analyst by considering the relationships between customers, employees, administrators, and external systems such as the DMV and payment processor. As the course progressed, my diagrams became more detailed and organized.

# What I Would Revise
If I could revise one part of my work, I would improve my UML diagrams by adding more detail and refining some of the relationships between objects and actors. While my diagrams met the project requirements, I have a better understanding now of how important it is to clearly represent every interaction and dependency within the system. I would also make the diagrams easier to read by improving their overall layout and organization.

# Interpreting the User’s Needs
Understanding the client’s needs was one of the most important parts of designing the DriverPass system. I carefully reviewed the business requirements and used them to guide every design decision. Each feature included in the system was based on solving a problem for either the customer or the business. Designing with the user in mind helps create software that is easier to use, more efficient, and better suited to the client’s goals. Meeting user requirements also reduces the need for costly redesigns later in development.

# My Approach to Software Design
When approaching software design, I start by gathering and understanding the customer’s requirements before creating any diagrams or models. I then break the system into smaller components and use UML diagrams to visualize how the system should function. Throughout this course, I learned the importance of functional, structural, and behavioral modeling because each provides a different perspective of the system. In future projects, I plan to continue gathering detailed requirements, creating multiple design models, reviewing my work for completeness, and considering feedback throughout the design process. These strategies will help me develop software that is both organized and focused on meeting the user’s needs.
